Грамматические правила

Здесь вы найдете основные грамматические правила английского языка, необходимые для 7 класса.

Present Simple (Настоящее простое время)

The Present Simple is used to express habits, general truths, repeated actions or unchanging situations, emotions and wishes:
- I smoke (habit); he works in London (unchanging situation); London is a large city (general truth)

It is also used to give instructions or directions:
- You walk for two hundred metres, then you turn left.

To express fixed arrangements, present or future:
- Your exam starts at 9.00.

To express future time, after some conjunctions: 'after', 'when', 'before', 'as soon as', 'until':
- He'll give it to you when you come next Saturday.

Past Simple (Прошедшее простое время)

The Past Simple is used to express actions completed in the past at a definite time. We often use time expressions like: yesterday, last week, an hour ago, in 2005, etc.
- I visited my grandparents yesterday.

It is also used to express a series of completed actions in the past:
- He woke up, had breakfast, and went to work.

To express habits or states in the past (often with 'used to'):
- She used to play the piano when she was young.

Present Perfect (Настоящее совершенное время)

The Present Perfect is used to express an action that happened at an unspecified time before now. The exact time is not important. You cannot use the Present Perfect with specific time expressions like: yesterday, an hour ago, last week.
- I have seen that movie before.

It is also used to express an action that started in the past and continues to the present:
- She has lived in London for five years (and she still lives there).

To express experiences or achievements:
- He has traveled around the world.

Future Simple (Будущее простое время)

The Future Simple is used to express future actions, predictions, or spontaneous decisions. We often use 'will' or 'shall' (for 'I' and 'we').
- I will go to the park tomorrow.
- She will probably pass the exam.
- Don't worry, I'll help you.

Modal Verbs (Модальные глаголы)

Modal verbs are auxiliary verbs that express necessity, possibility, ability, permission, or obligation. Common modal verbs include: can, could, may, might, must, should, will, would.
- You can speak English very well (ability).
- May I come in? (permission)
- You should study for your exams (obligation).
- It might rain tomorrow (possibility).

Future Continuous (Будущее продолженное время)

The Future Continuous is used to describe actions that will be in progress at a specific time in the future. The structure is 'will be' + verb-ing.
- At 10 AM tomorrow, I will be working.
- They will be traveling all next week.

Present Continuous (Настоящее продолженное время)

The Present Continuous is used to describe actions happening now, at the moment of speaking, or temporary actions. The structure is 'am/is/are' + verb-ing.
- I am studying English right now.
- She is currently living in London.
